Book I

The Gospel // What Is RetardMaxxing?

Somewhere along the way, you got smart. Too smart. You read the books, listened to the podcasts, built the second brain, optimized the morning routine — and somehow you're doing less than ever. You've got seventeen tabs open about how to start and zero things started. That's the disease. RetardMaxxing is the cure.

It started in a backyard. A middle-aged guy named Elisha Long, sitting in a lawn chair with a cigar and a grill going, talking straight into a camera for forty minutes at a stretch. No jump cuts. No ring light. No "smash that like button." Just a grown man telling you what your high school buddy would tell you if he loved you enough to be honest: you already know the answer. You knew it before you opened the spreadsheet. Trust your gut — if it feels wrong, it is. Now go do the thing.

Then it escaped the backyard. Marc Andreessen tweeted two words — "Current status: Retardmaxxing" — and called Long his new life coach. Jason Calacanis ran a thousand-post thread preaching zero rumination. The All-In pod chewed on it for an hour. Suddenly the most over-credentialed people in America were admitting what the guy with the cigar knew all along: the analysis was the procrastination. The optimization was the avoidance. The gut was right the whole time.

RetardMaxxing is not about being dumb. It's about refusing to let your galaxy brain talk you out of your life. Throw shit at the wall. See what sticks. Be unapologetically, radically yourself — the unpolished version, the one that says the thing and ships the thing and apologizes to nobody. The world belongs to people who move.

"Throw shit at the wall and see what sticks."
— Elisha Long, The Prophet, from the lawn chair
Book II

The 10 Commandments

  1. Thou shalt not ruminate.You've thought about it enough. You thought about it in the shower. You're done thinking. Go.
  2. Thou shalt trust thy gut.If it feels wrong, it is. Your gut ran the numbers before your brain opened Excel.
  3. Thou shalt ship it ugly.Version one is supposed to embarrass you. If it doesn't, you shipped too late.
  4. Thou shalt throw shit at the wall.The wall will tell you what sticks. The whiteboard never will.
  5. Thou shalt not build a second brain before using the first one.Your notes app is a graveyard. Delete the system, keep the action.
  6. Thou shalt say the thing.At the meeting. To her. To him. To your boss. The polished version you're rehearsing is worse.
  7. Thou shalt not wait for permission.Nobody's coming. That's the good news — it means it's all yours.
  8. Thou shalt keep zero regrets.You're allowed to be wrong. You're not allowed to wish you'd tried. Wrong is a lesson; waiting is a loss.
  9. Thou shalt be unapologetically thyself.The unpolished you is the one people actually trust. Retire the brand. Bring the guy.
  10. Thou shalt light the cigar.Metaphorically or otherwise. Sit down, slow down, and stop pretending life is an optimization problem. It's a grill and a lawn chair.

Book IX

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this offensive?

The name? Yeah, it's abrasive — that's not an accident, and we're not going to pretend otherwise. But hear the actual doctrine: the word is only ever pointed at yourself — at your own overthinking, your own spiral, your own seventeen open tabs. It is never, ever pointed at another human being, and it has nothing to do with anyone with a disability. Punching at anyone but your own inner committee is a violation of the whole philosophy.

If the name isn't for you, that's a gut feeling, and rule one is to trust it. No hard feelings, brother. The lawn chair will be here.

Is it really free to join?

Yes. Free forever. You give an email, we send a magic link, you click it, you're in the Congregation. No password, no fee, no credit card, no catch. We don't sell anything about you because we barely collect anything about you.

Do I have to buy the coin?

No. Read commandment two. The coin is a flag for the degens who want one — it is completely optional and completely separate from the community, which is free. If your gut says skip it, skip it. Anyone who tells you a memecoin is a sure thing is lying, and that's not this.

What do I actually DO? Like, step one?

Pick the thing you've been "researching" the longest. You know the one. Set a timer for ten minutes and take the first physical action toward it — send the email, buy the domain, make the call, put on the shoes. Then post it in the Congregation so the wall knows you did it.

Where did this come from?

Elisha Long's backyard, via YouTube. Then Marc Andreessen's March 2026 tweet, Jason Calacanis's June thread, the All-In podcast, and a Jeff Giesea Substack essay in between. In under a year it went from one lawn chair to the loudest anti-overthinking movement on the internet. Nobody planned it. Fitting.
